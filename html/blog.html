FOMO: Fear Of Missing Out
March 19 2023

HEY. It's me again. So, unfortunately, I will not be able to pump out another post before Monday, but I satisfied my weekly quota so it's fine. Who cares if I update it sporadically? I am determined to give some crumbs the few people who read this (and I will be part of the audience when I look back on this). p.s. I think I have become addicted to separating my sentences with break tags.

This spring break has been so hectic. I went out every single day of the week. Ironically, the only days where I could do nothing were the days leading up to the Monday of the break. And the only reason I don't have any time to myself is because of the fear of missing out.

During the daytimes, I went to friend's houses to play Mario Party, play board games (we spent 3 hours straight playing Here to Slay - Expansion Pack and it wouldn't have finished had we not forced someone to win; we were literally begging them to PLEASE take our hero cards) watch movies, hangout at downtown Houston, and eat at restaurants.

At nights (especially the ungodly hours), I would get looped into Discord voice chats because of the fun gaming sessions with new friends and silly conversations that could only occur with old friends.

Any real free time I had was spent working on projects, whether it was personal contributions to a community project or my class projects, and studying. Cue the sobbing emoji.

But would I change any part of how I did my spring break? Reflecting back on the amount of fun I had every day, I would say, in the grand scheme of things, no. I am genuinely glad and super grateful that I got to spend time with everyone. It's rare enough that I get a day to spend time with people who I've missed because both of us are busy with the grind or that person is out of town for school. I was actually talking about something similar with my brother today while we were at a family dinner (this is ironically the rarest form of interaction in my case). He was talking about how the only free time you'll ever have after you graduate is the weekend. You don't get holiday breaks. No long winter breaks, Thanksgiving breaks spring breaks, NO SUMMER, etc. And I thought, Wow. This is kind of depressing ngl. But what's the point in dreading the inevitability of that 9-5 work life? That's why I want to treasure every moment I have with other humans.

Friends are, most of the time, your backbone. I think the fear of missing out really hits during a break because everyone looks like they're having fun and obviously, you want to join in on it too or else you'll miss out on inside jokes, funny moments, and interesting friendships. Over the years, I've realized that I have a particular habit of multitasking online interactions. If I can text several people, join a voice call in one server, and join a Hyperbeam watch together session in another server, I *will* do it just so that I don't miss out on anything.

Is it always doable? No. Do I usually end up doing it? Ehh, 50/50 (STREAM CUPID). I do make sure to set limits on myself, especially when I have pressuring deadlines. Plus, too much social interaction is tiring. Anyways, the point is: it's hard to not get FOMO. My current solution is keeping to strict deadlines and muting all media noise. But I still find it hard to stay off Discord haha. Oh, and I still listen to brown noise which helps me avoid jamming out too hard. So, what about you guys? How hard is the FOMO addiction, if any, for y'all?

Music
March 15 2023

Goodness me, I am late to my own blog! Actually, this spring break has been so hectic as I've been busy with plans (social and academic). I'll talk about them in future posts (hopefully I can crank out two more posts before next Monday). But for now: MUSIC.

I am the type of person to always always be listening to something. I don't know if it's an ADHD thing (I am not diagnosed), but I HAVE to have either Spotify or YouTube running in the background in order for me to work. I wonder a lot on whether this is common. Because among my friends, I am the very few who enjoys background noise. Like right now, I am listening to soft brown noise on Spotify, and it's been playing the playlist since morning.

That doesn't mean I go on full focus mode whenever a media app is playing music though. I actually get distracted quite easily.

I'll spend 10 minutes scrolling through my YouTube recommendations to find the *right* or *perfect* song to listen to. Or I'll casually be listening to a playlist and a song I really like comes on, so I take a moment to jam out and end up getting distracted. So do I really need sound to focus, or am I just scared of boredom? I wish I knew the perfect mixture of a good study playlist back in high school because I don't think I did any productive studying when I was busy dancing to killer k-pop and early 2000's American pop songs.

Anyways, I feel like music is such a critical part to living and *existing* in life. Music has the power to make you feel certain emotions according to the genre you're listening to. If you're sad, and you listen to a sad song. Guess what? Your depression is amplified. And if you're feeling great while listening to your favorite upbeat song, you feel like you're on top of the world! There's not a day that I will not dance to a catchy song in my room and unleash my inner popstar persona. That's why I just find it so hard to believe that there are people who do not actually enjoy listening... to anything. Like I used to have a friend who refused to listen to music because it didn't "interest" her. It worked out for her though because she powered through her homework assignments like they were nothing and finished in the top 5%. But still, life without music is like living without seeing color. You're simply missing out on the vibrancy that music gives, in my opinion.

Anyways, I am not sure if I have a favorite genre. Sure, I love k-pop, but it has such a wide range of genres within it that not many people outside of k-pop realize. My all-time artists are IU, Heize, and SEVENTEEN. My newest favs are NewJeans and XG. And those artists vary quite widely from each other, so I'd say my musics tastes are diverse hehe. As for other music around the world, I think Keshi, OFFICIAL HIGE DANDISM, Fujii Kaze, and Nujabes remain to be my top favs.

If I had a comment section, I would ask y'all what you like to listen to. Maybe I'll implement it this summer; count on me to do it!

Go go go
February 19 2023

Heya! Hope all is going well for my readers. Today I want to talk about my time at my university's career fair. The day before, I went to a workshop that prepared you on what to do or say to the recruiters. I went there expecting to be kept to myself. But surprisingly, I made a few new friends! We were all nervous and jittery for the big day, so we practiced our lines and gave each other advice and emotional support. It was such a nice moment to share with lovely strangers that I am so glad I went. And then I went home and stayed up till 1 a.m. researching the companies I was interested in and printed out my notes to remind me of what special information they had and what I could tell them regarding my personal experiences.

I think everyone, at some point, has felt that career fairs are nerve-wracking. I remember stuttering my way through my first career fair last semester. I was not only unprepared for what the companies wanted or offered, but also just unsure of myself. I felt that I lacked a lot of experience compared to my peers. My resume was missing substantial substance. Who was I to go up to a recruiter and prove my worth when I had little to show? But at the same time, I think that alone motivated me to put myself out there. I knew that at the very least, I would gain practice from talking and putting myself out there. As I walked through the doors and into the bustling room, I took a deep breath and walked over to a booth and introduced myself. Surprisingly, the recruiters were very genuine and understanding. Since I told them it was my first time being at the fair, they took the lead and guided our conversation. By the end of the first hour, I felt my anxiety ease and slip away as I approached one recruiter after another.

Learning from that experience made me take the initiative this semester. I actually wasn't as nervous that morning. Instead, I met up with my new friend from last night and encouraged her to speak to her first recruiters. After talking to three recruiters together, I believed she was ready to do it alone. So we ended up going to the companies we were interested in for the next hour and a half. During that time, I found a company I was really interested in and shared my resume with them. I then talked to other recruiters to get more practice with talking. Overall, I think the career fair was a smooth process for me.

This time, I feel a little bit more confident about my skills and projects. But I also remind myself that I still have a long way to go and that my desired company probably won't reach out to me this year and that that is perfectly fine. It just means I have an extra year to hone my skills for the big and better things outside of my university :).

Put Yourself Out There
February 09 2023 - Boo!

Today was really fun and scary to me at the same time. The context is that I attended a club's meeting at my university for this semester. For introverts like me, social events are anxiety-inducing, especially when you go by yourself. And when you are already not a part of a clique, it's even harder to reach out to people. Towards the start of the meeting, I felt myself stressing out like crazy. While I was already acquainted with others at the club, I didn't know if I could will myself to be optimistic about my social prospects. I worried that I would end up sitting by myself without talking to anyone new. Approaching the building's elevators, I thought, I can't waste two hours here being alone. To my relief, I recognized a familiar person in the elevator whom I met previously at another club. At this point, my anxiety was reaching its peak, so I distracted myself with making small talk. We ended up talking some more on our way to the meeting's room. And thankfully, her and her friend sat next to me. I think what really helped pull us together was that we were both introverts navigating through a sociable environment at the time. We talked a lot about our fears and struggles in the CS world and having to "put ourselves out there." There were many times she psyched herself out from introducing herself to the club's officers. But eventually, with enough push and eagerness, she did it! And I felt very proud of her. Her determination was what inspired me to reach out as well. By the end of the event, I realized that everyone was quite friendly and approachable if you just came by and stay around. But that came with approaching them first and faking it till you make it. I found myself feeling more comfortable with the officers and speaking to acquaintances. My anxiety had shaken off and I was more confident in my speaking skills. So if you start feeling shy when you're attending a social gathering, you're definitely not alone! Many people have been or are in your place. Even if the first step is just introducing yourself, that's already quite big!
