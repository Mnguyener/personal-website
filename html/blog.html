Put Yourself Out There
February 09 2023 - Boo!

Today was really fun and scary to me at the same time. The context is that I attended my university's first general meeting for this semester. For introverts like me, social events are anxiety-inducing, especially when you go by yourself. And when you are already not a part of a clique, it's even harder to reach out to people. Towards the start of the meeting, I felt myself stressing out like crazy. While I was already acquainted with others at the club, I didn't know if I could will myself to be optimistic about my social prospects. I worried that I would end up sitting by myself without talking to anyone new. Approaching the building's elevators, I thought, I can't waste two hours here being alone. To my relief, I recognized a familiar person in the elevator whom I met previously at another club. At this point, my anxiety was reaching its peak, so I distracted myself with making small talk. We ended up talking some more on our way to the meeting's room. And thankfully, her and her friend sat next to me. I think what really helped pull us together was that we were both introverts navigating through a sociable environment at the time. We talked a lot about our fears and struggles in the CS world and having to "put ourselves out there." There were many times she psyched herself out from introducing herself to the club's officers. But eventually, with enough push and eagerness, she did it! And I felt very proud of her. Her determination was what inspired me to reach out as well. By the end of the event, I realized that everyone was quite friendly and approachable if you just came by and stay around. But that came with approaching them first and faking it till you make it. I found myself feeling more comfortable with the officers and speaking to acquaintances. My anxiety had shaken off and I was more confident in my speaking skills. So if you start feeling shy when you're attending a social gathering, you're definitely not alone! Many people have been or are in your place. Even if the first step is just introducing yourself, that's already quite big!
